Journal Entry

Part 1: How was the text selected?

I selected the text Animal Farm by George Orwell for many reasons.

It is a novel that I really enjoy reading as it is filled with symbolism and striking one liners.

The story presents a parallel between the change in the name of the farm and with society in general.

The paradoxical “All animals are equal, but some are more equal than others” reveals the digression of fairness within the Animal Farm as the tale unfolds.

In today’s world, where everyone wishes to be accepted and be considered equal, I find this novel to mirror the attitude of different people.

Furthermore, with significant events taking place in a fast paced narrative, I decided to map this novel.

Part 2: How was the narrative reduced to events?

Having read the book many times, I knew the essence of each chapter.

I was able to pick out the significant details which would help retain the flow of the novel.

Also since this book had many events which triggered the subsequent actions, I was able to map them as an ‘event and its consequence.’

Part 3: The process of map making

According to George Orwel, Animal Farm reflected the events leading up to the Russian Revolution of 1917 and then on into the Stalinist era of the Soviet Union.

The Soviet Union, he believed, had become a brutal dictatorship, built upon a cult of personality and enforced by a reign of terror.

In order to translate that on to the map, I positioned the animal farm in in the city of Saint Petersburg where the Russian Revolution began.

I used a topographic map with layers that included World Hillshade, World Boundaries and Places in order to add another aesthetic dimension.

The events are mapped along the boundary to pictorially represent the paw of an animal. This symbolises the footsteps of change.
